# Dig-Site R02 — RG, Universality, and Sharp Thresholds Outside Equilibrium Physics

## TL;DR
- **RG/universality outside physics is mostly metaphor, with two rigorous islands:** the Park–Pham theorem (JAMS 2024) makes threshold/expectation-threshold universality a genuine theorem in probabilistic combinatorics, and Bahri–Dyer–Kaplan–Lee–Sharma (PNAS 2024) give partly-controlled derivations of neural scaling-law exponents. The RG-to-deep-learning "exact mapping" (Mehta–Schwab 2014) and phenomenological-RG-to-brain claims (Meshulam–Bialek 2019) are suggestive analogies that fail specific quantitative controls.
- **Kahn–Kalai is settled (Park–Pham, JAMS 2024); the residual log factor is provably tight for some spanning structures (Hamilton cycle, perfect matching) and provably absent for others (square of a Hamilton cycle).** The stronger "second" Kahn–Kalai conjecture remains OPEN (best bound today: p_c = O(p_E·log³n)), and the log-gap prediction provably FAILS for down-sets (gap can be polynomial in n).
- **Self-organized criticality is real as a dynamical mechanism in specific tuned/absorbing-state models but is over-claimed empirically; power-law fitting alone is not evidence** (Clauset–Shalizi–Newman 2009; Broido–Clauset 2019), and cortical "avalanche criticality" is contested by strong null models (Touboul–Destexhe; Wilting–Priesemann; Morrell–Nemenman–Sederberg).

## 1) State of the art 2023–2026 (primary sources first)

### A. Kahn–Kalai and sharp thresholds — the cleanest rigorous case
- **Park & Pham, "A proof of the Kahn–Kalai conjecture," arXiv:2203.17207 (v1 31 Mar 2022; v2 13 Apr 2023); published J. Amer. Math. Soc. 37(1):235–243 (2024), DOI 10.1090/jams/1028.** THEOREM: for any increasing property F on finite X, p_c(F) = O(q(F)·log ℓ(F)), where q is the expectation threshold and ℓ the maximum size of a minimal member of F. As shown in the paper, log|X| can be replaced by log(max size of a minimal member). This is the rigorous universality-of-thresholds statement — "the best possible easy lower bound on p_c is not too far from the truth."
- **Simplifications / sharpenings (all primary preprints):** Tran & Vu, "A short proof of Kahn–Kalai conjecture," arXiv:2303.02144 (reduces the proof to ≈1 page; shows the constant K ≈ 3.998 is achievable under the current method); Bell, "The Park–Pham Theorem with Optimal Convergence Rate," arXiv:2210.03691 (optimal ε-dependence as ε→0); Park & Vondrák, "A simple proof of the non-uniform Kahn–Kalai conjecture," arXiv:2306.12576 (works for non-uniform product measures, near-optimal even for p near 1, clean bound p_c ≤ 4q_c·log₂(7ℓ)).
- **Survey:** Perkins, "Searching for (sharp) thresholds in random structures: where are we now?," arXiv:2401.01800 (Jan 2024, Current Events Bulletin, JMM 2024). Frames Park–Pham together with Ding–Sly–Sun's proof of the satisfiability conjecture for large k.
- **Consequences already cashed out:** Shamir's problem (perfect matchings in random k-uniform hypergraphs) and K_r-factors, originally solved by Johansson–Kahn–Vu (Random Struct. Alg., 2008, arXiv:0803.3406), now follow from Frankston–Kahn–Narayanan–Park (Annals of Math. 194:475–495, 2021) and Park–Pham; also bounded-degree spanning trees, and "robust"/spread versions of Dirac-type theorems.

### B. RG / universality in machine learning
- **Bahri, Dyer, Kaplan, Lee & Sharma, "Explaining neural scaling laws," arXiv:2102.06701; published PNAS 121 (2024), DOI 10.1073/pnas.2311878121 (edited by Yuhai Tu; received Aug 2023, accepted Mar 2024).** Identifies four scaling regimes — variance-limited and resolution-limited, each for data and model size. Contains a genuine theorem (variance-limited scaling follows from the existence of a well-behaved infinite-data/infinite-width limit) and a data-manifold hypothesis (resolution-limited exponents set by the intrinsic dimension of a smooth data manifold), derived exactly for random-feature teacher–student models.
- **Roberts, Yaida & Hanin, "The Principles of Deep Learning Theory," arXiv:2106.10165 (Cambridge University Press, 2022).** Uses RG/effective-field-theory machinery (criticality of initialization hyperparameters, Schwinger–Dyson equations) to derive controlled 1/width corrections to the infinite-width limit. Rigorous within its perturbative regime; the RG here is a real calculational tool, not a metaphor.
- **Peraza Coppola, Helias & Ringel, "Renormalization group for deep neural networks: Universality of learning and scaling laws," arXiv:2510.25553 (29 Oct 2025; v2 1 Jan 2026).** RG framework for learning curves of weakly non-linear ("non-lazy") networks trained on power-law data; introduces "scaling intervals" replacing scaling dimensions; a Gaussian-Process-like UV fixed point governs large-data universality. Very recent; not yet independently replicated — treat as a promising primary result, not consensus.
- **Metaphor line (foundational):** Mehta & Schwab, "An exact mapping between the Variational Renormalization Group and Deep Learning," arXiv:1410.3831 (2014) — maps stacked RBMs to Kadanoff variational RG on the Ising model.

### C. RG on networks
- **García-Pérez, Boguñá & Serrano, "Multiscale unfolding of real networks by geometric renormalization," Nature Physics 14:583–589 (2018).** Geometric RG via embedding into a hidden metric space; finds geometric scaling in real scale-free networks and generates self-similar scaled-down replicas.
- **Villegas, Gili, Caldarelli & Gabrielli, "Laplacian renormalization group for heterogeneous networks," Nature Physics 19:445–450 (2023).** Diffusion/Laplacian-based RG that handles the small-world "coexisting scales" obstacle field-theoretically.
- **Higher-order Laplacian RG, Nature Physics (2025), DOI 10.1038/s41567-025-02784-1** — extends the Laplacian scheme to hypergraphs via cross-order Laplacians. These are controlled mathematical constructions (real coarse-graining operators), though "universality" claims about real data remain empirical.

### D. RG/criticality in biology (neuroscience)
- **Meshulam, Gauthier, Brody, Tank & Bialek, "Coarse Graining, Fixed Points, and Scaling in a Large Population of Neurons," Phys. Rev. Lett. 123:178103 (2019)** (preprint "Coarse-graining and hints of scaling in a population of 1000+ neurons," arXiv:1812.11904). The phenomenological RG (PRG): coarse-grain by iteratively summing the most-correlated pairs of neurons (real-space) or by projecting out low-variance covariance eigenmodes (momentum-space). Applied to **a population of 1000+ cells in mouse hippocampus**, it reports power-law dependences of static/dynamic quantities on coarse-graining scale **over two decades**, with reproducible exponents, and coarse-grained distributions approaching **a fixed non-Gaussian form**.
- **Recent (2023–2026) PRG applications:** Morales, Di Santo & Muñoz, "Quasiuniversal scaling in mouse-brain neuronal activity stems from edge-of-instability critical dynamics," PNAS 120(9):e2208998120 (2023); Nature Communications s42003-023-05001-y (2023), applying PRG to human fMRI/dMRI; a 2025 PRX Life paper (Barzon–Busiello–Nicoletti circle) linking mouse-cortex scaling to task performance. Also newer theory: "Renormalization group analysis reveals universality classes of stochastic spiking neuron networks," arXiv:2301.09600, building an actual (non-phenomenological) RG for stochastic leaky-integrate-and-fire networks.

## 2) Open problems ranked by tractability for a single-GPU / Colab run

**Tier 1 — directly touchable, little/no GPU:**
1. **Empirical tightness of the Kahn–Kalai log factor in G(n,p).** Numerically locate the threshold window for fixed spanning structures (perfect matching, Hamilton cycle, square of a Hamilton cycle) and compare to the expectation threshold. n up to a few thousand is feasible; pure combinatorial simulation.
2. **Reproduce/stress-test the Clauset–Shalizi–Newman power-law protocol.** Take published "SOC" datasets and neural-avalanche data; run MLE exponent estimation + Kolmogorov–Smirnov goodness-of-fit + log-normal likelihood-ratio tests (the CSN `powerlaw` toolchain). Cheap and decisive.
3. **PRG spurious-scaling check.** Reproduce Nicoletti–Suweis–Maritan (Phys. Rev. Research 2:023144, 2020): run PRG on a supercritical contact process and on shuffled/surrogate data to see whether scaling signatures persist off-criticality. Small CPU simulations.

**Tier 2 — feasible on one GPU:**
4. **Neural scaling-law exponents vs. data-manifold dimension** (Bahri et al. prediction): train small networks / random-feature models on synthetic manifolds of controlled intrinsic dimension d and check whether the resolution-limited exponent tracks the predicted dependence on d.
5. **RBM/RG "exact mapping" quantitative test** (following the 2023 "A Deep Dive into the Connections Between the Renormalization Group and Deep Learning in the Ising Model," arXiv:2308.11075): train an RBM on Ising configurations and check whether the induced flow reproduces the correct correlation-length / scaling-dimension trend quantitatively, not just qualitatively.

**Tier 3 — harder, borderline for Colab:**
6. **Geometric-RG self-similarity on real networks.** Embedding + coarse-graining large graphs is memory-bound; only medium networks fit in Colab RAM.

## 3) Strongest critiques, failed replications, live controversies (who disputes what)

- **RG ≈ deep learning "exact mapping."** Lin & Tegmark ("Why does deep and cheap learning work so well?," 2016) gave a counterexample; Schwab & Mehta replied (arXiv:1609.03541); Koch-Janusz & Ringel argued that training RBMs by minimizing KL divergence does NOT perform RG, and proposed a real-space-mutual-information (RSMI) scheme instead. The 2023 "Deep Dive" (arXiv:2308.11075) concludes there are strong QUALITATIVE connections but "we do not have enough evidence to rule in favor of this connection existing on a quantitative level"; for the long-range Ising model the RBM flow does NOT converge to the correct spin/energy scaling dimensions. VERDICT (inference): productive analogy, not an established theorem.
- **Phenomenological RG in neuroscience.** Nicoletti, Suweis & Maritan (Phys. Rev. Research 2:023144, 2020; arXiv:2001.04353) showed only SOME PRG observables tie scaling to criticality, and that even simple models with spectral degeneracy produce spurious scaling. Their abstract: "the method proves to be able to distinguish the critical regime from the supercritical one. However, some scaling features persist in the supercritical regime, at least for a finite system, as we see in a contact process above λc" (the contact process "displays an absorbing phase transition at λ=λc between a silent and an active state"). Morrell, Nemenman & Sederberg ("Neural criticality from effective latent variables," eLife 2024;12:RP89337; arXiv:2301.00759) show a model "with at least about five latent variables can produce power laws under the coarse-graining analysis" and that "avalanche criticality is observed without fine-tuning." VERDICT: PRG scaling is necessary-not-sufficient evidence for criticality.
- **Self-organized criticality / avalanches.** Touboul & Destexhe (PLoS ONE 5:e8982, 2010; and "Power-law statistics and universal scaling in the absence of criticality," Phys. Rev. E, 2017) showed thresholded stochastic processes produce spurious power laws, and that non-critical models (Brunel network, stochastic surrogate) pass the standard "criticality" tests — a control that was absent from Fontenele et al. (Phys. Rev. Lett. 122:208101, 2019). Wilting & Priesemann ("Inferring collective dynamical states from widely unobserved systems," Nat. Commun. 9:2325, 2018) argue the cortex is not critical but in a subsampling-robust "reverberating" regime: using their multistep-regression estimator, "m̂ ranged from 0.963 to 0.998 (median m̂ = 0.98, for a bin size of Δt = 4 ms)" across monkey PFC, cat visual cortex, and rat hippocampus (0.94 < m̂ < 0.991) — close to, but reliably below, the critical m = 1.
- **Scale-free networks.** Broido & Clauset, "Scale-free networks are rare," Nature Communications 10:1017 (2019): applying the CSN tests to a corpus of **928 network data sets**, they find "strongly scale-free structure is empirically rare, while for most networks, log-normal distributions fit the data as well or better than power laws"; only a small minority (roughly a handful of technological/biological networks) are strongly scale-free. Contested by Barabási-school researchers; Petter Holme's companion piece "Rare and everywhere" (Nat. Commun. 10:1016, 2019) offers a balanced reading.
- **Power-law fitting generally.** Clauset, Shalizi & Newman, "Power-law distributions in empirical data," SIAM Review 51:661–703 (2009): the foundational methodological critique — least-squares log-log fitting misestimates exponents; use MLE + KS goodness-of-fit + likelihood-ratio comparison to alternatives (log-normal, exponential, power-law-with-cutoff). Many claimed power laws are not statistically preferred over these alternatives, especially without data spanning several decades.

## 4) The 5 most load-bearing references (one line each on WHY)

1. **Park & Pham, JAMS 37:235–243 (2024) [arXiv:2203.17207].** The theorem that turns threshold "universality" from heuristic into proved mathematics — the anchor of the entire site.
2. **Clauset, Shalizi & Newman, SIAM Review 51:661–703 (2009).** The methodological hinge: it defines what counts as evidence for a power law and retroactively demotes much of the SOC/scale-free literature.
3. **Bahri, Dyer, Kaplan, Lee & Sharma, PNAS 121 (2024) [arXiv:2102.06701].** The most controlled account of WHY neural scaling laws are power laws, cleanly separating provable (variance-limited) from hypothesized (resolution-limited) regimes.
4. **Meshulam et al., PRL 123:178103 (2019).** The concrete, testable instantiation of "RG for real biological data" — and the exact object the 2020–2024 critiques dissect.
5. **Nicoletti, Suweis & Maritan, Phys. Rev. Research 2:023144 (2020).** The cleanest demonstration that the PRG can manufacture criticality signatures off-criticality — the null-generator for the whole neuroscience-RG program.

## 5) For each experiment idea in (2): the NULL-CASE first

1. **KK log-factor tightness.** NULL: If the simulated Hamilton-cycle/perfect-matching threshold window sits at the expectation threshold (order 1/n) rather than at log n/n — i.e., no log gap appears — the "tightness" demonstration has failed (almost certainly a finite-size/simulation artifact, since the log gap is a proven asymptotic fact here). Conversely, the idea is only validated once you see the gap for the Hamilton cycle AND its ABSENCE for the square of a Hamilton cycle (whose true threshold and expectation threshold both scale as 1/√n).
2. **CSN power-law protocol.** NULL: If, on synthetic Pareto data you KNOW is power-law, the MLE+KS+LRT pipeline fails to prefer the power law, your pipeline is broken and no downstream claim is trustworthy. The productive demotion you are hunting is the mirror image: a known log-normal (mis)classified as power-law by naïve log-log fitting but correctly rejected by the full pipeline.
3. **PRG spurious scaling.** NULL: If PRG exponents cleanly DIFFER between a critical contact process and its supercritical/shuffled surrogate, PRG is a valid criticality detector and the "spurious scaling" worry is demoted. Only if the surrogates reproduce the scaling (as Nicoletti et al. report near, but not far from, λ_c) is the critique upheld.
4. **Scaling-law exponent vs. manifold dimension.** NULL: If the measured resolution-limited exponent does NOT move when you vary the intrinsic dimension d of the synthetic data manifold, the Bahri et al. mechanism is wrong (or your run is not in the resolution-limited regime), and the idea is dead.
5. **RBM/RG mapping.** NULL: If the RBM flow fails to reproduce the correct Ising correlation-length / scaling-dimension trend (as already found for the long-range Ising case in arXiv:2308.11075), the "exact mapping" stays metaphor. This null is already partially realized in the literature — so the experiment's upside is narrow.

## 6) Adjacent fringe claims to steer around (and why each is fringe)

- **"The brain is (proven to be) critical / sits exactly at a phase transition."** Fringe as stated: the strong version rests on power-law avalanche fits that fail controls; the defensible version is "near-critical / reverberating / quasi-critical" (median branching ratio ≈ 0.98, below 1), a much weaker claim.
- **"Deep learning IS renormalization" (literal identity).** Fringe: the exact-mapping claim fails quantitative tests (long-range Ising, KL-vs-RG objection); only structural analogy survives.
- **"Most real networks are scale-free (power-law degree) as a universal law."** Fringe post-2019: empirically rare across 928 networks; log-normal often fits as well or better.
- **"Matching a power-law exponent proves a common SOC universality class."** Fringe: universality-class assignment needs more than one matching exponent; crackling-noise scaling relations (Sethna) are necessary-not-sufficient and are reproduced by non-critical latent-variable and stochastic-modulation models (Morrell–Nemenman–Sederberg; Touboul–Destexhe).
- **"Dragon-king" extreme events as a routine SOC signature.** Steer around treating it as established: Aschwanden's own solar/stellar analysis found dragon-king outliers in only 4 of 25 datasets, concluding most flare distributions remain scale-free/single-mechanism.
- **"1/f noise everywhere ⇒ criticality."** Fringe: 1/f noise has many non-critical generative mechanisms (catalogued at length in Sornette's textbook); it is not a criticality diagnostic on its own.

## Caveats and hygiene notes

**Theorems vs. conjectures (explicit):**
- Park–Pham / Kahn–Kalai (p_c = O(q·log ℓ)) — **THEOREM** (JAMS 2024). Confidence: HIGH.
- **Where the log factor is TIGHT — THEOREM-backed empirical fact:** for Hamilton cycles the expectation threshold is order 1/n while the true threshold is log n/n (Komlós–Szemerédi, *Discrete Math.* 43:55–63, 1983; Ajtai–Komlós–Szemerédi, 1985); the same log n gap holds for perfect matchings (Erdős–Rényi, 1964). These are the motivating tight cases in Kahn–Kalai (*Combin. Probab. Comput.* 16:495–502, 2007). The "sharp version of Talagrand" paper (arXiv:2412.03540) states plainly that "the logarithmic factor in the gap between p_E(H) and p_c(H) is known to be tight in several specific examples." Confidence: HIGH.
- **Where the log factor is REMOVABLE — THEOREM:** Kahn, Narayanan & Park, "The threshold for the square of a Hamilton cycle," *Proc. Amer. Math. Soc.* 149(8):3201–3208 (2021), arXiv:2010.08592: "we show that p = 1/√n is the threshold for the random graph G_{n,p} to contain the square of a Hamilton cycle" — matching the expectation threshold with NO log factor. Fixed-size subgraphs (triangles, fixed cliques) are likewise sharp. Confidence: HIGH.
- **The "second" Kahn–Kalai conjecture — still OPEN (CONJECTURE).** It predicts, for containing a fixed graph H, p_c(H) = O(p_E(H)·log n). The current best is Dubroff, Kahn & Park, "On the 'second' Kahn–Kalai Conjecture," arXiv:2508.14269 (2025), which proves p*_E(H) = O(p_E(H)·log²n) and hence, via Frankston–Kahn–Narayanan–Park, **p_c(H) = O(p_E(H)·log³n)** — progress, not resolution. A companion (arXiv:2510.02005, 2025) resolves special cases (cliques, cycles, trees). NOTE: a distinct 2022 paper of the same title by Mossel, Niles-Weed, Sun & Zadik (arXiv:2209.03326) proves only a *modified* version using a redefined subgraph expectation threshold p̃_E — do not conflate the two.
- **Down-sets — the log-gap prediction provably FAILS (THEOREM).** Gunby, He & Narayanan, "Down-set thresholds," *Random Structures & Algorithms* 63(2):442–456 (2023), arXiv:2112.08525: "there exist down-sets with polynomial gaps between their thresholds and expectation-thresholds; in particular, the logarithmic gap predictions of Kahn–Kalai and Talagrand … do not apply to down-sets" (concrete witness: triangle-free graphs on [n], with a √n-scale separation). Simpler proof and extension to F-free graphs: Warnke, *RSA* 64(4):807–813 (2024), arXiv:2310.11662. This is the sharpest "clean negative" at the site: the universality of the log gap is a property of UP-sets specifically. Confidence: HIGH.
- **Neural scaling laws:** variance-limited regime is a theorem under stated assumptions; resolution-limited regime is a HYPOTHESIS (manifold-dimension mechanism). Confidence that exponents are explained by manifold dimension: MODERATE.
- **SOC "as a real mechanism":** a genuine mechanism in specific tuned/absorbing-state and sandpile models (contact process, Bak–Tang–Wiesenfeld, forest-fire), but a contested EMPIRICAL claim in brains and astrophysics. The empirical status is best summarized as "power law ≠ criticality unless controls and scaling relations are jointly satisfied."

**Confidence grades (empirical claims):** Kahn–Kalai status and its tight/removable examples — HIGH. CSN methodological critique and "scale-free networks are rare" — HIGH. "PRG can produce spurious scaling off-criticality" — HIGH (multiple independent demonstrations). "Cortex is reverberating (m≈0.98), not exactly critical" — MODERATE (method- and subsampling-dependent). "Neural scaling-law exponents set by manifold dimension" — MODERATE. "RG genuinely (quantitatively) explains deep learning" — LOW.
