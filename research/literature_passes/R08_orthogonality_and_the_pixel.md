# R08 · "Orthogonality & the Pixel" — Dig-Site Literature Pass

## TL;DR
- **d=6 MUBs: OPEN, leaning strongly negative.** No proof exists that more than 3 MUBs are impossible in dimension 6; the maximum is only provably bounded between 3 and 7. Zauner's conjecture (max = 3) is backed by strong, mutually consistent numerical evidence but no rigorous infeasibility certificate. A 2025 McNulty–Weigert Comment invalidated three published theorems that had claimed structural restrictions on hypothetical four-MUB sets, so the negative case is now *less* established than the prior literature suggested.
- **SICs / Zauner: conditionally reduced to number theory in 2025, still not unconditionally proven.** Appleby–Flammia–Kopp (7 Jan 2025, arXiv:2501.03970) reduced Zauner's conjecture in *all* dimensions to two number-theoretic conjectures (the order-1 abelian Stark conjecture for real quadratic fields + a Shintani–Faddeev special-value identity). Both remain open. Unconditional exact SICs exist only in an explicit finite list of dimensions (1–21, 24, 28, 30, 31, 35, 37, 39, 43, 48, 124, 323, plus scattered higher).
- **Gleason in d=2: genuinely fails — and this is a clean negative, not a gap.** The Born rule is *not* forced for a qubit by projective measurements alone; explicit non-Born frame functions exist. What survives is Busch's POVM/effect version (valid for all d≥2) at the cost of a stronger additivity assumption. "2-dimensional probability" in the pure-projective sense is genuinely underdetermined.

## Key Findings
- **Q1 (MUB-6): honest answer is OPEN.** It is a theorem that 7 MUBs exist when d is a prime power, and d=6 is the smallest non-prime-power. It is a theorem that ≥3 MUBs exist in d=6. Whether a 4th exists is unresolved; the maximum lies provably in {3,…,7}. "Max = 3" is a *conjecture* (Zauner), supported by several independent numerical methods that all fail to find a fourth basis. CITED, not inferred.
- **Q2 (SICs): existence proven in finitely many dimensions; universal existence is a conjecture, with a dramatic 2025 *conditional* result.** The AFK construction is the headline development and is a conditional theorem, not an unconditional one.
- **Q3 (Gleason d=2): fails, with explicit counterexamples; repaired only by strengthening assumptions.** Settled mathematics; the sole "controversy" (De Zela) is essentially resolved against the strong claim.

## Details

### 1. Mutually Unbiased Bases in dimension 6 (2023–2026)

**Primary sources (dated):**
- **D. McNulty & S. Weigert, "Mutually Unbiased Bases in Composite Dimensions — A Review," arXiv:2410.23997 (v1 31 Oct 2024; v2 26 Mar 2026; published as Quantum 10, 2051, 1 April 2026).** The current authoritative survey. States plainly that it remains unknown whether complete sets of MUBs exist in any non-prime-power dimension, including 6 and 10, and presents fourteen mathematically equivalent formulations of the existence problem plus a comprehensive summary of analytic, computer-aided, and numerical results. CITED.
- **D. McNulty & S. Weigert, "Comment on 'Product states and Schmidt rank of mutually unbiased bases in dimension six'," arXiv:2504.13067 (17 April 2025).** A load-bearing negative result. It shows that Lemma 11(v) Part 6 of Chen & Yu (J. Phys. A 50, 475304, 2017) contains an erroneous proof step, and that this error propagates to invalidate three later theorems: (i) that no Hadamard matrix in a MU-quadruple can have more than 22 real entries [Liang–Hu–Sun–Chen–Chen, Linear Multilinear Algebra 69, 2908 (2021)]; and (ii)–(iii) restrictions on the number of 2×2 Hadamard sub-blocks of H2-reducible matrices [Liang–Hu–Chen–Chen, QIP 18, 352 (2019); Chen–Liang–Hu–Chen, QIP 20, 353 (2021)]. The Comment provides an explicit one-parameter counterexample family M₆(a). Net effect: several claimed structural constraints on hypothetical four-MUB sets are no longer established. (The authors note some co-authors of the "≤22" theorem privately communicated a Lemma-1-independent proof that awaits publication.) CITED.
- **Numerical evidence against a fourth basis:** Three independent numerical approaches — see-saw optimisation, non-linear SDP, and Monte Carlo — recast the MUB question as a Bell-inequality optimization and all fail to find four MUBs in d=6, converging to the "four most distant bases" of Raynal–Lü–Englert (Phys. Rev. A 83, 062303, 2011). Source: "Three numerical approaches to find mutually unbiased bases using Bell inequalities," Quantum 6, 778 (17 Aug 2022); the same Monte Carlo work suggests no more than 3 MUBs in d=10. CITED. The authors explicitly caution that heuristic numerics "can never provide a rigorous proof of the non-existence of MUBs."

**Connection to complex Hadamard matrix classification.** Any basis MU to the standard basis in ℂ⁶ corresponds to a 6×6 complex Hadamard matrix. The classification of complex Hadamard matrices is complete only up to order 5; order 6 is the smallest open case. The conjectured picture (Szöllősi, arXiv:1008.0632, 2010) is that all 6×6 complex Hadamards are captured by Szöllősi's generic four-parameter family G₆⁽⁴⁾, Karlsson's three-parameter degenerate family K₆⁽³⁾, and Tao's isolated spectral matrix S₆⁽⁰⁾, which Szöllősi conjectures "form an exhaustive list of complex Hadamard matrices of order 6." This exhaustiveness is itself a CONJECTURE, evidenced by "defect" computations: in the center-manifold flow analysis of arXiv:1609.02829, "save for S₆⁽⁰⁾ the defect is always found to be four." If true, the conjecture would map the MUB-6 search onto a bounded four-parameter landscape. The Fourier family F₆ has been rigorously excluded from any MU-quadruple (Jaming–Matolcsi–Móra 2010, combined with the Matolcsi–Weiner Delsarte-type LP bound, Open Syst. Inf. Dyn. 22, 1550001, 2015). CITED.

### 2. SIC-POVMs and the Appleby–Flammia–Kopp / Stark program

**The number-theoretic story.** Appleby, Flammia, McConnell & Yard (Found. Phys. 47, 1042, 2017; Acta Arith. 192, 211, 2020) empirically discovered that SIC fiducial vectors in dimension d generate specific abelian (ray class) extensions of the real quadratic field ℚ(√((d+1)(d−3))), tying SIC existence to Hilbert's 12th problem. Kopp (IMRN 2021, arXiv:1807.05877) sharpened this: SIC overlap phases are Galois conjugate to square roots of Stark units, and he produced the first exact solution in dimension 23 (with conjectural constructions in d = 5, 11, 17, 23 for odd primes ≡ 2 mod 3). CITED.

**The 2025 headline — Appleby, Flammia & Kopp, "A Constructive Approach to Zauner's Conjecture via the Stark Conjectures," arXiv:2501.03970 (v1 7 Jan 2025; v2 17 Mar 2025; 177 pages).** This is a CONDITIONAL THEOREM. Verbatim from the abstract: "We prove our construction gives a valid SIC in every case assuming two conjectures: the order 1 abelian Stark conjecture for real quadratic fields and a special value identity for the Shintani-Faddeev modular cocycle." The construction produces a "ghost SIC" from real-multiplication values of the Shintani–Faddeev modular cocycle, Galois conjugate to an actual SIC; source code is released (github.com/sflammia/Zauner.jl). It remains a preprint with no confirmed peer-reviewed journal publication as of mid-2026, and the authors solicit feedback. Expert framing (e.g. A. Bandeira's Randomstrasse101/ETH notes, Dec 2025) treats the *logical reduction* (Stark conjectures ⇒ Zauner) as sound while stressing "an unconditional proof of Zauner's Conjecture remains unavailable." CITED (partly via subagent).

**Status of the two conditioning conjectures — both OPEN:**
- The **order-1 (rank-one) abelian Stark conjecture** is a theorem only for base field ℚ (Dirichlet) and imaginary quadratic fields (Stark, ~1980). It is UNPROVEN for real quadratic fields — precisely the case AFK25 requires. (The Brumer–Stark conjecture, a *different* refinement, was proven away from p=2 by Dasgupta–Kakde 2023; this does not settle the rank-one abelian case for real quadratic fields.) CITED.
- The **Shintani–Faddeev special-value identity** is a newer, largely conjectural statement from Kopp's companion paper "The Shintani–Faddeev modular cocycle: Stark units from q-Pochhammer ratios," arXiv:2411.06763 (Nov 2024, rev. May 2025). Kopp proves a refinement of Shintani's Kronecker limit formula there, but the full identity used to establish idempotency of the fiducial projector is conjectural. CITED (via subagent).

**Which dimensions are PROVEN (unconditional exact SICs)?** The canonical published set of dimensions with rigorous exact algebraic constructions is **d = 1–21, 24, 28, 30, 31, 35, 37, 39, 43, 48, 124, 323** (Kopp, IMRN 2021, restating the accumulated literature). Additional exact Weyl–Heisenberg solutions with higher symmetry were given by Scott ("SICs: Extending the list of solutions," arXiv:1703.03993, 2017) specifically in "d = 124, 143, 147, 168, 172, 195, 199, 228, 259 and 323," together with a putatively complete list for d ≤ 90 and a Zauner-symmetry solution for every d ≤ 121. Numerical (non-exact) fiducials are known more broadly: per the Fuchs–Hoang–Stacey review (arXiv:1703.07901) "solutions in all dimensions up to 151, and in several cases beyond that, as large as dimension 844," with Grassl's extended catalogue adding numerical fiducials at N ≤ 193 and N = 204, 224, 255, 288, 528, 725, 1155, 2208.

CRUCIAL DISTINCTION: the large-dimension Stark-unit constructions are themselves CONDITIONAL on the same number-theoretic conjectures — they are *not* unconditional existence proofs. Grassl (CodEx talk, 26 Oct 2021) reports the Stark-units method has been "successfully applied in 34 dimensions: d = 7, 12, 19, 28, 39, 52, 67, 84, 103, 124, 147, 172, 199, 259, 292, 327, 403, 487, 628, 787, 844, 964, 1027, 1228, 1299, 1447, 1684, 1852, 2404, 2707, 4099, 5779, 19603, and 39604." For the prime n²+3 family, Appleby–Bengtsson–Grassl–Harrison–McConnell (J. Math. Phys. 63, 112205, 2022; arXiv:2112.05552) state "such structures are shown to exist in 13 prime dimensions of this kind, the highest being p = 19603"; the Bengtsson–Grassl–McConnell follow-up (J. Math. Phys. 66, 082202, 2025; arXiv:2403.02872) reaches d = 39604. CITED (partly via subagent).

**Special note on d=6 for SICs:** Unlike MUBs, SICs are unproblematic in d=6 — Grassl gave an exact algebraic SIC in dimension 6 ("On SIC-POVMs and MUBs in dimension 6," 2004) and Zauner covariance was verified. So d=6 is a pain point for MUBs but not for SICs. CITED.

### 3. Gleason's theorem in dimension 2

**What fails.** Gleason's theorem (A. M. Gleason, J. Math. Mech. 6, 885, 1957) proves that for a Hilbert space of dimension ≥ 3, every countably-additive probability assignment on projections (frame function) has the Born form Tr(ρP). It is a THEOREM that this FAILS at d=2. The reason: with only projective (von Neumann) measurements on a qubit, each rank-1 projector P appears in exactly one orthogonal pair {P, I−P}, so additivity imposes only one equation per basis, with no "intertwining" of measurements. This constraint is too weak to force continuity/linearity. CITED.

**Explicit counterexamples (CITED):**
- Discontinuous: g(|0⟩⟨0|)=0, g(|1⟩⟨1|)=1, g=½ otherwise, satisfies g(P)+g(I−P)=1 for every projective measurement but is not of trace form (arXiv:1808.08091, Wright & Weigert).
- Smooth (continuous) non-Born family: p_s(P_ψ)=½[1+(n_z)³] on the Bloch sphere — additive on orthogonal qubit projectors but nonlinear (arXiv:1611.00613, comment on De Zela). This shows continuity alone does not rescue the theorem for d=2.
- On the real circle S¹: g(θ)=1+cos(nθ) for n≡2 mod 4 is a nonnegative Gleason function of weight 2 for ℝ² that is not a quadratic form (arXiv:2001.06738).

**What replaces it.**
- **Busch's theorem (Paul Busch, Phys. Rev. Lett. 91, 120403, 2003; independently Caves–Fuchs–Manne–Renes 2004).** Extends the domain from projections to all effects (positive operators 0 ≤ E ≤ I, the outcomes of POVMs). Any generalized probability measure additive over effects summing to I is forced to v(E)=Tr(ρE) — and this holds for ALL d ≥ 2, including the qubit, with a proof simpler than Gleason's. THEOREM. The price: the additivity premise is stronger (ranging over POVM effects, not just orthogonal projections), which some argue is less physically innocent. CITED.
- **Gleason-type theorems (Wright & Weigert, arXiv:1808.08091, 2019).** Systematically identify the smallest measurement set beyond pure PVMs that restores a Gleason-type conclusion for the qubit. They show 2-outcome POVMs alone remain insufficient; measurements with ≥3 outcomes (e.g. projective-simulable measurements PSM₂) are necessary. This pins the exact boundary of the d=2 failure. THEOREM/analysis. CITED.
- **De Zela's disputed claim.** F. De Zela argued a Gleason-type theorem for qubits could be recovered from projective measurements alone by adding continuity + "eigenstate" assumptions. This was disputed: the smooth counterexample above (arXiv:1611.00613) and follow-ups show the added assumptions either fail or covertly amount to the stronger orthogonal-additivity condition (via Gudder's theorem). The consensus is that pure-projective d=2 Gleason cannot be salvaged without a stronger premise. CITED / INFERRED (consensus reading).

**Is there a meaningful "2-dimensional probability" that survives?** INFERRED conclusion from the cited results: yes, but only in the POVM/effect sense (Busch), not the pure-projective sense. If one insists on projective measurements only, qubit probability is genuinely underdetermined — the Born rule is one choice among a continuum. This is the crux of the site: orthogonality is too *sparse* in d=2 to generate probability, and you must enrich the measurement structure to recover it. It dovetails with the Kochen–Specker theorem also requiring d ≥ 3.

## Ranked open problems by single-GPU / Colab tractability

**Tier A — genuinely touchable on small compute:**

**1. Numerical MUB-constellation search in d=6 (probing between 3 and 4 MUBs).** Recast as a smooth non-negative objective (Bell-functional of Quantum 6, 778, or the Butterley–Hall 𝔑_{d,N} function) and minimize with GPU-parallel random restarts / Adam. Cheaply reproduces known results and lets you push on constellations like {5,3,3,3}₆ that would imply a fourth basis.
- **NULL-CASE (stated first):** If your optimizer reliably drives the objective to *exact* zero for the {5,3,3,3}₆ constellation from many random seeds, that falsifies "max = 3." The *expected* outcome — merely reproducing the known nonzero floor (the "four most distant bases" value) — teaches NOTHING new: heuristic non-convergence is not a proof of nonexistence (explicitly stated by the Quantum 6, 778 authors). Demotion trigger: you cannot recover the known d=5 (success) vs d=6 (failure) contrast, meaning the implementation is unreliable.

**2. Independent re-verification of the invalidated Chen–Yu / Liang et al. claims.** The McNulty–Weigert Comment (2504.13067) supplies the explicit counterexample family M₆(a). A small exact / interval-arithmetic computation can independently confirm that the vector v need not have two vanishing components, corroborating the invalidation — and can test whether a Lemma-1-free proof of the "≤22 real entries" theorem is reconstructible.
- **NULL-CASE (stated first):** If interval arithmetic shows the disputed components *do* vanish for all M₆(a), the Comment's counterexample collapses and the original theorems stand — killing this line. Demotion: computation inconclusive at achievable precision.

**Tier B — touchable but bottlenecked:**

**3. Numerical SIC fiducial search in a moderate dimension.** Gradient descent on the frame potential from random fiducials, GPU-accelerated, double/quad precision. Routine to ~150; higher needs high precision.
- **NULL-CASE (stated first):** Failure to find a fiducial proves nothing — Zauner predicts one exists and the search is simply hard. (Illustrative: the standard numerical random search for d=5779 came up empty after 55065 trials / 17.69 CPU-years, per arXiv:2112.05552; the Stark-unit method later *succeeded* at that dimension, underscoring that heuristic-search failure is uninformative.) Only SUCCESS is informative. Demotion: cannot reproduce a known numerical fiducial (e.g. d=48) to published precision → broken pipeline.

**4. Exact-solution "integer relation" recovery for a known numerical SIC.** Take a high-precision numerical fiducial and use PSLQ/LLL to recognize algebraic entries and the number field — the method behind many published exact solutions. Colab can run mpmath/PARI for moderate degree.
- **NULL-CASE (stated first):** PSLQ failing at achievable precision/degree is *expected* in high dimensions (degrees explode) and says nothing about existence. Demotion: failure to recover a known low-d exact field (e.g. the d=8 Hoggar lines) indicates a precision/setup error.

**Tier C — largely out of reach on small compute:**

**5. Gröbner-basis infeasibility certificate for four MUBs in d=6.** The "clean" route to actually PROVING max = 3. Documented to exhaust 16 GB RAM on the smallest interesting constellations: {5,3³}₆ requires a Gröbner basis of an ideal generated by 61 degree-4 equations in 90 real variables (arXiv:1006.0093). Lasserre SDP hierarchies are asymptotically complete but blow up per level.
- **NULL-CASE (stated first):** You will almost certainly run out of memory before termination (documented). A genuine "1 ∈ ideal" would be a nonexistence proof — but do not expect it. Demotion: immediate, on any realistic Colab RAM budget.

**6. Complex Hadamard order-6 classification (proving G₆ ∪ K₆ ∪ S₆ exhaustiveness).** Research-grade symbolic problem; small compute can only test the defect = 4 property on samples, not prove exhaustiveness.
- **NULL-CASE (stated first):** Finding a 6×6 complex Hadamard with defect ≠ 4 outside the known families would overturn the conjecture; the expected outcome is that you only ever see defect 4, which merely re-corroborates. Demotion: sampling cannot settle a classification.

## Adjacent fringe claims to steer around
- **"SIC-POVMs exist in all dimensions" (arXiv:0707.3002, 2007).** Fringe/erroneous: conflates real spherical 2-designs with the complex equiangular-line requirement. The community uniformly treats Zauner's conjecture as open; do not cite this as a proof. INFERRED fringe status from consensus + subagent flag.
- **"Four MUBs in d=6 was proven impossible in 2025" (as asserted on Grokipedia's MUB page).** Fringe/inaccurate secondary source. No such proof exists; primary sources (McNulty–Weigert 2024/2026) explicitly say the problem is open. Steer around AI-generated encyclopedic summaries here.
- **Over-strong QBism claims that the SIC "urgleichung" REPLACES the Born rule or single-handedly reconstructs all of quantum theory.** The urgleichung (Fuchs–Schack) is a genuine, interesting SIC-based rewriting of the Born rule as a deformation of the law of total probability, and it is legitimate research; but claims that it constitutes a *completed* reconstruction of QM are aspirational — the reconstruction program is explicitly incomplete by its own proponents' admission. Relevant context (SICs as the "missing" informationally-complete reference measurement) but not a settled foundation.
- **Claims that Gleason's theorem alone derives the Born rule for qubits (De Zela-style).** Disputed and not accepted without a stronger additivity/continuity premise. Treat any "Born rule proven for the qubit from projections alone" claim as fringe.

## Caveats
- The MUB-6 maximum is bounded in {3,…,7}; "3" is a CONJECTURE (Zauner), not a theorem. Every numerical method here is heuristic and cannot prove nonexistence — only existence (by exhibiting a fourth basis).
- AFK25 is a CONDITIONAL theorem and a preprint; it does not unconditionally prove Zauner's SIC conjecture, and both underlying number-theoretic conjectures are open. No confirmed peer-reviewed publication and no major popular-press feature were found as of mid-2026 (absence of evidence, not confirmed negative).
- Exact-SIC dimension lists differ slightly between surveys (some include d=23 as exact, some 195; catalogues extend variously). The minimal well-established published set is quoted above; d=53 is exact but was reported unpublished. Large-dimension Stark constructions (up to 39604) are conditional.
- The Gleason-d=2 material is settled mathematics; the only "controversy" (De Zela) is effectively resolved against the strong claim.
- Grading summary: prime-power MUB existence, d=6 having ≥3 MUBs, the finite exact-SIC list, and Gleason's d≥3 theorem / d=2 failure are HIGH confidence. "Max MUBs in d=6 = 3" and "no 4th MUB in d=10" are MODERATE (strong numerical evidence, no proof). The Szöllősi Hadamard-exhaustiveness picture and the AFK reduction being ultimately provable are MODERATE-to-LOW (conjectural).
