# OMPU / "The Anomaly Dig" — Prior-Art Probe: Percolation-Gated Graph Navigation, Two-Field (Repulsor/Conductance) Dynamics, and the "Scar" Naming Overlap

## TL;DR
- **No prior art found for the exact combination.** No paper models knowledge-graph navigation as a percolation problem with an explicit phase transition in an edge-weight threshold *and* uses that threshold to gate exploration-vs-exploitation. The pieces exist separately; the combination appears genuinely open (moderate-high confidence).
- **No prior art for the two-field formulation.** Repulsive potential fields are standard in robotics path planning and have been ported to *latent/embedding* navigation, but treating repeatable failure-classes as an explicit repulsor field co-existing with a separate attractive "conductance/findings" field on an abstract concept graph has no located precedent (moderate confidence). The closest is failure-aware agent memory, which stores failures as constraints/records, not as a repulsive potential.
- **"Scar" bridge is a clean negative.** No paper connects the physics notion of a scar (Heller eigenfunction scars or quantum many-body scars) to associative memory / retrieval / agent memory as a memory *mechanism*. The nearest neighbor is He & Lubchenko, "Knowledge as a Breaking of Ergodicity" (Neural Computation 37(4):742–792, 2025), which frames ML memory as classical ergodicity-breaking but does not use "scar." You are free to use the term but should explicitly distinguish yourselves.

## Key Findings

**Q1 (percolation-gated graph navigation): OPEN — no exact prior art.** All three ingredients exist in isolation but are never combined as specified:
- Percolation on graphs with edge-weight/bond thresholds and explicit phase transitions is a mature field (network science).
- GraphRAG explicitly frames retrieval as an exploration/exploitation trade-off — but gates it with heuristics (community filtering, MCTS, PageRank), never a percolation threshold.
- The single closest artifact is a non-peer-reviewed industry blog (Percolation Labs / Saoirse Amarteifio) that uses percolation as a *design metaphor* for RAG — but with no phase-transition math and no exploration/exploitation gate.

**Q2 (failure-classes as repulsor potentials): OPEN — no exact prior art for the two-field formulation.** Artificial Potential Fields (Khatib, "Real-Time Obstacle Avoidance for Manipulators and Mobile Robots," Int. J. Robotics Research 5(1):90–98, 1986) are the canonical attractive-goal + repulsive-obstacle formulation and have been ported to neural/latent navigation. Failure-aware agent memory (e.g., "negative knowledge" banks) is a live 2025–2026 topic but treats failures as records/constraints, not as a repulsive field on a concept graph.

**Q3 ("scar" naming): CLEAN NEGATIVE.** The scar↔memory bridge does not exist in the literature; use the term but distinguish from both physics senses and from "Knowledge as a Breaking of Ergodicity."

## Details

### 1) State of the art 2023–2026 (primary sources first)

#### 1A. Percolation on networks / phase transitions (physics & network science) — mature, foundational
- **Bond percolation on weighted graphs with a giant-component threshold** is rigorously established. *David Aldous, "The Incipient Giant Component in Bond Percolation on General Finite Weighted Graphs," Electronic Communications in Probability 21:1–9 (2016), DOI 10.1214/16-ECP21 (arXiv:1604.06741)* — edges open at random Exponential times with rates set by edge weights; the paper proves that "the time at which a giant component starts to emerge is weakly concentrated around its mean." **[Theorem-level result; confirmed peer-reviewed.]** This is the mathematically cleanest precedent for "edge-weight threshold → connectivity phase transition," but it is not about retrieval/navigation.
- **Edge-weight-thresholded bond percolation applied to a real semantic/citation network** appears in *"A diachronic study of historiography" (arXiv:1807.01795)*, which literally computes connected components after "removal of edges with weight below t," describing it as "a form of bond percolation where edges are removed deterministically according to their weight." **[Close on mechanism, but the domain is bibliometrics, not navigation/RAG, and there is no exploration/exploitation gate.]**
- **Explosive percolation / k-core percolation** (discontinuous transitions): reviewed in *"Recent advances and open challenges in percolation" (arXiv:1404.5325)*; k-core and explosive percolation work associated with **R. M. D'Souza, J. Nagler, and collaborators** (e.g., Chen & D'Souza, PRL 2011). Recent k-core percolation on spatially embedded networks: *Gallos et al., "Nucleation phenomena and extreme vulnerability of spatial k-core systems," Nature Communications (2024)*; *"Delayed threshold and spatial diffusion in k-core percolation," Communications Physics (2025)*. **[High confidence; foundational network-science, not tied to knowledge-graph retrieval.]**
- **The Havlin/Newman/D'Souza statistical-physics lineage** is active but I found **no** instance where this community applied percolation specifically to knowledge-graph *retrieval/navigation with an exploration/exploitation gate*. **[Inference from absence: moderate confidence.]**

#### 1B. Percolation in the RAG/knowledge-graph area — metaphor and mechanism, but not the gate
- **Graph Percolation Embeddings (GPE):** *"River of No Return: Graph Percolation Embeddings for Efficient Knowledge Graph Reasoning" (arXiv:2305.09974, 2023)* uses "percolation" as a directional message-passing metaphor ("the river of no return, flowing from the source... never going backward"). **This is NOT statistical-physics percolation: there is no phase transition, no edge-weight threshold gating, and no exploration/exploitation regime switch.** Closeness: terminological only.
- **Percolation Labs / Saoirse Amarteifio, "Generative retrieval and the concept of percolation in statistical physics" (Substack, Feb 5, 2025):** explicitly invokes the percolation threshold, subcritical/critical/supercritical regimes, and connectivity for RAG design ("the percolation threshold is the critical value at which the system undergoes a transition from being mostly disconnected to becoming percolated"). **This is the single closest published articulation of the core intuition** — but it is (a) a non-peer-reviewed blog by a former physics PhD, (b) purely conceptual (no model, no phase-transition measurement), and (c) does not formalize a threshold that gates exploration vs. exploitation. Closeness: highest on *intuition*, lowest on *rigor*.
- **Spreading-activation RAG with explicit thresholds:** *"Leveraging Spreading Activation for Improved Document Retrieval in Knowledge-Graph-Based RAG Systems" (arXiv:2512.15922, 2025)* runs spreading activation over a weighted entity subgraph with an activation threshold τ_a and relationship-weight threshold τ_r (reporting τ_a=0.5, τ_d=0.45, τ_r=0.5 as working values). **This is the closest ML artifact combining (b) edge-weight thresholds with graph navigation** — but the thresholds are activation cutoffs, not a percolation phase transition, and they do not switch exploration/exploitation regimes. Closeness: high on "edge-weight threshold on a knowledge graph," nil on "percolation phase transition as an explicit gate."

#### 1C. GraphRAG exploration/exploitation — the trade-off is named, but gated by heuristics not percolation
- *"Deep GraphRAG: A Balanced Approach to Hierarchical Retrieval and Adaptive Integration" (arXiv:2601.11144, 2026)* explicitly names "the exploration-exploitation trade-off in graph navigation" and addresses it with a three-stage hierarchical filter (inter-community filtering → community refinement → entity-level search). **No percolation threshold.**
- *"Reasoning by Exploration (RoE)" (arXiv:2510.07484, 2025)*; *"GraphScout" (arXiv:2603.01410, 2026)*; *"RANGER: Repository-level Agent for Graph-Enhanced Retrieval" (arXiv:2509.25257, 2025)* — the last uses MCTS-inspired traversal that "balances exploration and exploitation" over a repository knowledge graph. *"Odin: Multi-Signal Graph Intelligence for Autonomous Discovery in Knowledge Graphs" (arXiv:2603.03097, 2026)* explicitly contrasts "discovery of unknown patterns" vs. Microsoft GraphRAG's known-question answering and reviews Personalized PageRank / random walks as exploration methods. **None uses a percolation phase transition as the gate.**
- Microsoft GraphRAG itself is the reference baseline: *Darren Edge, Ha Trinh, Newman Cheng, Joshua Bradley, Alex Chao, Apurva Mody, Steven Truitt, Robert Osazuwa Ness & Jonathan Larson (Microsoft Research), "From Local to Global: A Graph RAG Approach to Query-Focused Summarization," arXiv:2404.16130 (April 24, 2024)*.
- **Verdict Q1:** The novel claim — *percolation threshold on a knowledge graph used as an explicit exploration/exploitation gate* — has **no located prior art**. The exploration/exploitation framing is ubiquitous; the percolation-threshold-as-gate is absent. **(Moderate-high confidence this is genuinely open; a clean, valuable negative.)**

#### 1D. Spreading activation / ACT-R lineage (cognitive precedent for thresholded graph retrieval)
- **Collins & Loftus (1975)** spreading-activation theory and **Anderson's ACT-R** (Anderson 1993; Anderson et al. 2004) formalize activation spreading through a weighted semantic network with **retrieval thresholds** and decay (ACT-R retrieves the most-active chunk if it exceeds a fixed threshold). This is a decades-old precedent for "threshold-gated traversal of a concept graph," but the threshold is an activation cutoff, not a percolation transition, and there is no exploration/exploitation regime switch. Recent neural revival: *"Predictive Associative Memory: Retrieval Beyond Similarity Through Temporal Co-occurrence" (arXiv:2602.11322, 2026)* explicitly positions itself as supplying the "missing" learning mechanism for spreading activation.

#### 1E. Artificial Potential Fields and ports to abstract/latent navigation (for Q2)
- **Khatib (1986)** is the origin of APF: attractive potential at the goal + repulsive potentials around obstacles, agent descends the summed potential. *O. Khatib, "Real-Time Obstacle Avoidance for Manipulators and Mobile Robots," Int. J. Robotics Research 5(1):90–98 (1986), DOI 10.1177/027836498600500106* — canonical and heavily cited (8,700+). Known failure mode: local minima where attractive and repulsive forces cancel.
- **Ports to latent/embedding space (not physical space):** *"One-4-All: Neural Potential Fields for Embodied Navigation" (arXiv:2303.04011, 2023)* performs APF-style navigation where "potential computations occur over the latent space X, i.e. the embeddings of the agent and goal RGB observations." *"Bootstrapped LLM Semantics for Context-Aware Path Planning" (arXiv:2511.11967, 2025)* and *"Semantic potential field for mobile robot navigation using grid maps" (ETRI Journal, 2025)* add semantic/LLM information to potential fields. **These are the closest ports of repulsive potentials toward "semantic" navigation — but they remain grounded in physical/embodied navigation, not abstract concept-graph traversal.**
- **RL with learned potential/negative-reward landscapes:** *"From Scalar Rewards to Potential Trends: Shaping Potential Landscapes for Model-Based RL (SLOPE)" (arXiv:2602.03201, 2026)* constructs "informative potential landscapes" instead of scalar rewards. This is the nearest RL analogue of a learned field over states, but it is an optimistic *attractive/exploration* shaping, not a distinct repulsor field over failure-classes.

#### 1F. Failure-aware / negative-knowledge agent memory (for Q2)
- *"Negative Knowledge as Failure-aware Shared Memory for AutoResearch" (arXiv:2606.21024, 2026)* introduces a "negative knowledge memory layer" that "turns each failed attempt into a reusable record" so that "a scientific project [pays] for a failed route only once, then redirect[s] later attempts toward routes not yet ruled out." **This is the closest agent-memory precedent for treating failures as a distinct structural class** — but failures are stored as *records/constraints* consulted before acting, NOT as a repulsive potential field with navigation dynamics. Closeness: high on "failures as a distinct memory class," nil on "repulsor potential."
- Related 2025–2026 agent-memory work: *Mem0* (Chhikara et al., 2025, hybrid vector+graph with ADD/UPDATE/DELETE), *A-MEM*, *MemGPT* (Packer et al., 2024), *"Is Agent Memory a Database?" (arXiv:2605.26252)*, *"MemFail: Stress-Testing Failure Modes of LLM Memory Systems" (arXiv:2605.26667)*, *"AMA-Bench" (arXiv:2602.22769)* (causality-graph memory). None implements a repulsive field over failure-classes.
- **Verdict Q2:** The **two-field formulation** (repulsive potential on failures + separate attractive conductance on findings, as two distinct physical fields on an abstract concept graph) has **no located precedent**. APF gives the two-field structure but in physical space; failure-aware memory gives the failure-class structure but as records. Their synthesis onto a knowledge graph appears open. **(Moderate confidence; a genuine white space, though the constituent ideas are individually well-worn.)**

#### 1G. The "scar" naming overlap (Q3)
- **Physics origin (single-particle):** *E. J. Heller, "Bound-State Eigenfunctions of Classically Chaotic Hamiltonian Systems: Scars of Periodic Orbits," Phys. Rev. Lett. 53:1515–1518 (1984), DOI 10.1103/PhysRevLett.53.1515* — "certain unstable periodic orbits are shown to permanently scar some quantum eigenfunctions as ℏ→0, in the sense that extra density surrounds the region of the periodic orbit." **[Foundational quantum-chaos observation.]**
- **Many-body scars:** *C. J. Turner, A. A. Michailidis, D. A. Abanin, M. Serbyn & Z. Papić, "Weak ergodicity breaking from quantum many-body scars," Nature Physics 14(7):745–749 (2018), DOI 10.1038/s41567-018-0137-5 (arXiv:1711.03528)* — ergodicity "can be weakly broken by a different mechanism, arising from the presence of special eigenstates in the many-body spectrum that are reminiscent of quantum scars in chaotic non-interacting systems." The PXP/Rydberg experimental line (Bernien et al., Nature 2017) followed. Reviews: *Serbyn, Abanin & Papić, Nature Physics 17:675–685 (2021)*; *Moudgalya, Bernevig & Regnault, Rep. Prog. Phys. 85:086501 (2022)*. Scars "retain memory of the initial state" — but strictly in the quench-dynamics/revival sense.
- **The bridge to ML memory does NOT exist.** A dedicated sub-probe across ~10 query framings found no paper connecting scars (either sense) to associative memory / Hopfield / retrieval / knowledge graphs / agent memory *as a memory mechanism*.
- **Nearest neighbor to steer around:** *Yang He & Vassiliy Lubchenko, "Knowledge as a Breaking of Ergodicity," Neural Computation 37(4):742–792 (2025), DOI 10.1162/neco_a_01741 (arXiv:2412.16411)* frames ML memory storage as ergodicity breaking: "training amounts to sampling a free energy surface in the form of a library of distinct bound states, each of which breaks ergodicity." This is one conceptual step from scars (which are *weak* ergodicity breaking) but it does NOT use "scar" or cite QMBS/Heller, and it works in a classical/thermodynamic (RBM) framing. Also adjacent: quantum-Hopfield papers (e.g., *A Quantum Hopfield Associative Memory... on an Actual Quantum Processor*, arXiv:2105.11590; *Phase diagram of quantum generalized Potts-Hopfield networks*, arXiv:2109.10140) are hardware implementations, not scar analogies; and *"Uncovering Quantum Many-body Scars with Quantum Machine Learning" (arXiv:2409.07405; npj Quantum Information, 2025)* uses ML to *detect* scars (reverse direction). A further near-collision: *"Nonergodic delocalized paramagnetic states in quantum neural networks" (arXiv:2205.00623)* applies ETH/ergodicity-breaking language to a quantum Hopfield model but does not mention scars and treats the effect as a spectral novelty, not a memory mechanism.
- **Verdict Q3:** Clean negative. The term "scar" is safe to adopt but you should explicitly distinguish (a) from Heller quantum-chaos scarring, (b) from quantum many-body scars, and (c) from the ergodicity-breaking-as-memory framing of He & Lubchenko (2025).

### 2) Open problems ranked by tractability for a single-GPU / Colab run

1. **(Most tractable) Measure whether a growing knowledge graph exhibits a bond-percolation transition in an edge-weight threshold.** Build a weighted concept graph (from embeddings/co-occurrence), sweep an edge-weight threshold t, and measure giant-component size vs. t. Directly computable in networkx/igraph on Colab, no GPU needed.
2. **Test whether the percolation regime (sub/critical/super) predicts optimal exploration vs. exploitation.** Define a retrieval policy that switches regimes at the empirical threshold; compare against fixed policies on a multi-hop QA benchmark (e.g., MuSiQue, HotpotQA). Feasible on one GPU with a small LLM.
3. **Implement a two-field navigation dynamic on the concept graph:** an attractive conductance field on findings + a repulsive potential on failure-nodes; measure retrieval quality vs. single-field and vs. tabu/blocklist baselines. Moderate effort.
4. **(Least tractable) Establish a theoretical link between the percolation threshold and the exploration/exploitation optimum** (analytic result). This is a conjecture, likely beyond a Colab run; would need mean-field/generating-function analysis.

### 3) Strongest critiques, failed replications, live controversies
- **Percolation thresholds on real (finite, structured) networks are "smeared," not sharp.** *"Smeared phase transitions in percolation on real complex networks" (arXiv:1810.00735)* argues that observed discrepancies are due to real-network structure ("inhomogeneities in the topological distribution of the order parameter"), not just finite size. **This is the strongest critique of any claim that a knowledge graph has a clean, usable percolation threshold** — the transition may be too smeared to gate on.
- **APF local-minima problem** is a well-known, decades-old failure mode (Khatib critiques; "narrow corridor" problem). Any two-field navigation inherits this: the agent can be trapped where attractive and repulsive forces cancel. Disputed remedies (navigation functions, vortex fields, wall-following) are themselves contested.
- **Agent-memory abstraction is itself a documented failure mode:** *"Useful Memories Become Faulty When Continuously Updated by LLMs" (arXiv:2605.12978, 2026)* warns that LLM-driven abstraction over agent memory causes "silent regressions" and recommends preserving raw evidence and gating abstraction. This bears directly on storing failure-classes as abstracted structural objects.
- **Explosive percolation "discontinuity" controversy:** early claims of a genuinely discontinuous transition (Achlioptas processes) were later shown to be continuous in the thermodynamic limit (Riordan & Warnke; da Costa et al.). Anyone invoking "explosive" behavior on a knowledge graph must avoid this trap.

### 5) Null-cases (written BEFORE claiming promise)
- **Experiment 1 (percolation transition exists):** NULL = giant-component size grows *smoothly* with the edge-weight threshold with no identifiable critical region (smeared transition per arXiv:1810.00735). If there is no knee, there is no threshold to gate on → idea demoted.
- **Experiment 2 (regime predicts explore/exploit):** NULL = a policy that switches at the empirical percolation threshold performs no better (within run-to-run variance) than a fixed threshold or a simple degree/similarity heuristic on multi-hop QA. If the percolation point carries no policy signal, the core claim fails.
- **Experiment 3 (two-field beats baselines):** NULL = the repulsor-field-on-failures policy performs no better than a plain tabu list / blocklist / negative-sampling baseline. If a repulsive *field* adds nothing over a simple *set* of forbidden nodes, the "field" framing is decorative.
- **Experiment 4 (theory link):** NULL = no analytic relationship between the threshold and the explore/exploit optimum survives even in a mean-field toy model → remains a conjecture, not a theorem.

### 6) Adjacent fringe claims to steer around
- **"Semantic percolation" in Relational Frame Theory / contextual behavioral science** (contextualscience.org blog: "Percolation Thresholds and Complex Relational Responding"): claims cognition undergoes a literal percolation "phase transition" from "discrete relational frames to networked relational behavior." This is a loose metaphor with no rigorous model; cite only as an unrelated coincidence of terminology, not as support.
- **Grokipedia** entries (e.g., on Modern Hopfield networks) surfaced in search — not a citable primary source; use the underlying arXiv papers (Ramsauer et al. 2021; Krotov & Hopfield 2016) instead.
- **Over-broad "quantum brain / quantum cognition" claims** that invoke quantum scars for consciousness/memory — fringe; the scar↔memory bridge does NOT exist in reputable literature, so any such claim is unsupported.
- **Marketing-grade GraphRAG "discovery" claims** (some vendor blogs) that assert percolation-like emergence without measurement — treat as unvalidated.

## Recommendations
1. **Stage 1 (do first, cheapest):** Run Experiment 1 on a real embedding-derived concept graph. Pre-register the null (smooth curve = no threshold). Benchmark against the arXiv:1810.00735 smearing critique — if you see a smeared rather than sharp transition, pivot to using the *inflection region* rather than a sharp critical point, and report honestly. **Threshold to proceed:** a visible knee/inflection with a finite-size-scaling trend consistent with a transition.
2. **Stage 2 (if a knee exists):** Run Experiment 2 with a small LLM on MuSiQue/HotpotQA. **Threshold that would change your conclusion:** the percolation-gated policy must beat the best fixed-threshold baseline by a margin exceeding run-to-run variance across seeds.
3. **Stage 3 (two-field):** Only build the repulsor field if it clears the tabu-list baseline (Experiment 3 null). If it doesn't beat a blocklist, keep failures as a set, not a field.
4. **Naming:** Adopt "scar" but add a one-paragraph disambiguation distinguishing from Heller scars, QMBS, and the He & Lubchenko (2025) ergodicity-breaking framing. This pre-empts the most likely reviewer objection.
5. **Positioning:** Frame the contribution as the *combination* (percolation-threshold-as-explore/exploit-gate + two-field failure/finding dynamics), citing spreading-activation RAG (arXiv:2512.15922) and Deep GraphRAG (arXiv:2601.11144) as the closest existing work you extend, and Aldous (2016) as the mathematical grounding.

## Caveats
- **Confidence grades:** Q1 "open" — moderate-high confidence (the exploration/exploitation framing is heavily searched, so absence is meaningful). Q2 "open" for the two-field synthesis — moderate confidence (constituent ideas are common; synthesis unlocated). Q3 clean negative — high confidence, corroborated by a dedicated sub-probe.
- **Theorems vs. conjectures:** Aldous (giant-component concentration) and Heller/QMBS results are **theorems/established physics**. The claim that a percolation threshold optimally gates exploration/exploitation is a **conjecture** with no located proof.
- **Inference vs. citation:** That the Havlin/Newman/D'Souza percolation community has NOT touched knowledge-graph retrieval-with-gating is an **inference from absence**, not a cited statement.
- **Unverified specifics:** Several 2026 preprint arXiv IDs (2601.11144, 2603.01410, 2603.03097, 2606.21024, 2602.03201, 2602.11322) were read via search snippets; treat exact IDs as provisional and verify before publication. Confirmed/enriched identifiers: Aldous (DOI 10.1214/16-ECP21), Khatib (DOI 10.1177/027836498600500106), Turner et al. (DOI 10.1038/s41567-018-0137-5, arXiv:1711.03528), Heller (DOI 10.1103/PhysRevLett.53.1515), He & Lubchenko (DOI 10.1162/neco_a_01741, arXiv:2412.16411), Microsoft GraphRAG (arXiv:2404.16130).
- **No fabricated citations:** Where I was unsure a reference existed or of its exact identifiers, I flagged it explicitly above. The scar↔memory negative is an absence-of-evidence result; a poorly-indexed or very recent (late-2026) source could exist, but none surfaced across ~10 query framings plus a dedicated sub-probe.
