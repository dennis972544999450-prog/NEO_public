# R03 — Entropy: Leaks Between Scales (Thermalization, Area Laws, and Their Controversies)

## TL;DR
- **ETH is on very firm ground as the generic rule** (textbook-standard, with strong 2022–2025 refinements via "full ETH"/free probability); its cleanest *violations* — quantum many-body scars in the PXP/Rydberg model — are real and reproducible, but their thermodynamic-limit fate and microscopic origin remain **open**. The MBL *regime* is robust, but whether a true MBL *phase* exists in the infinite-size/infinite-time limit is **genuinely unresolved** as of 2025.
- **The Page curve from islands/replica wormholes is a controlled semiclassical calculation, not a full theorem**: within 2D JT gravity the island rule is *derived* from the gravitational path integral (Euclidean replica wormholes), but the derivation assumes the gravitational path integral is well-defined, uses replica-symmetric saddles, and — crucially — in solvable models computes *ensemble-averaged* quantities. Whether this resolves unitarity for a *single* black hole is still debated (factorization puzzle).
- **Area laws are proven only in 1D** (Hastings 2007 theorem, gapped local Hamiltonians) and in **2D only for frustration-free, locally-gapped systems** (Anshu–Arad–Gosset 2022); the general higher-dimensional area law is an **open conjecture**. The entanglement→geometry program (Ryu–Takayanagi, Lewkowycz–Maldacena, tensor networks, Van Raamsdonk) is a mix of one controlled holographic derivation and several physically-motivated conjectures/slogans.

## Key Findings

### Q1 — ETH, scars, and the MBL controversy

**ETH status (2024–2026): strongly confirmed as the generic mechanism (high confidence).** ETH (Deutsch 1991; Srednicki 1994; Rigol–Dunjko–Olshanii, Nature 452, 854, 2008) is textbook-standard. It is a **hypothesis/ansatz**, not a theorem for generic local Hamiltonians, but it is overwhelmingly supported numerically and has recently been *sharpened*: Pappalardi, Foini & Kurchan (PRL 129, 170603, 2022) connected higher-order ETH correlations to **free probability**, and Pappalardi, Fritzsch & Prosen (PRL 134, 140404, 2025) verified "full ETH" via free cumulants in local spin chains and Floquet circuits by exact diagonalization. This is an active, small-compute-friendly frontier (see also Fava–Kurchan–Pappalardi, "Designs via free probability," Phys. Rev. X 15, 011031, 2025).

**Cleanest ETH violations — quantum many-body scars (high confidence they exist; origin open).** Turner, Michailidis, Abanin, Serbyn & Papić (Nature Physics 14, 745, 2018) identified scars in the PXP model (effective description of Rydberg blockade), following the 51-atom experiment of Bernien et al. (Nature 551, 579, 2017). Scars are a small number of non-thermal, low-entanglement eigenstates causing persistent revivals from the |Z₂⟩ state. **The microscopic origin of PXP scars remains elusive** (explicitly stated in the primary literature): candidate explanations include an approximate su(2) spectrum-generating algebra, proximity to integrability (Khemani–Laumann–Chandran 2019; and the Floquet-integrability connection of Giudici–Surace–Pichler, PRL 133, 190404, 2024), and unstable periodic orbits in a TDVP semiclassical picture. 2025 developments: Kerschbaumer, Ljubotina, Serbyn & Desaules (PRL 134, 160401, 2025) found scars beyond PXP in longer-range Rydberg simulators; Ivanov & Motrunich (PRL 134, 050403, 2025) constructed exact volume-entangled scar states. Some models (AKLT, spin-1 XY) admit *exact* analytic scar towers (theorem-level for those specific constructions; Moudgalya–Bernevig–Regnault review, Rep. Prog. Phys. 85, 086501, 2022).

**The MBL controversy — is an MBL phase established? Honest answer: NO, it is not settled (high confidence about the state of the debate).**
- **The skeptics:** Šuntajs, Bonča, Prosen & Vidmar ("Quantum chaos challenges many-body localization," Phys. Rev. E 102, 062144, 2020) argued from spectral (Thouless-time) diagnostics that finite chains remain ergodic at any disorder in the thermodynamic limit. Sels & Polkovnikov (Phys. Rev. E 104, 054105, 2021; Phys. Rev. X 13, 011041, 2023) argued nonergodicity at strong disorder is a large but transient crossover.
- **The flagship review:** Sierant, Lewenstein, Scardicchio, Vidmar & Zakrzewski, "Many-Body Localization in the Age of Classical Computing" (Reports on Progress in Physics 88, 026502, 2025; arXiv:2403.07111). Its verdict is deliberately agnostic: the MBL *regime* (finite-size, finite-time non-thermalization at strong disorder) is well-established, but — quoting the abstract — "the central question remains unanswered: under what conditions does the MBL regime give rise to an MBL phase, in which the thermalization does not occur even in the asymptotic limit of infinite system size and evolution time?" **Persistent finite-size drifts toward ergodicity prevent unambiguous extrapolation**; the review concludes the topic "remain[s] a captivating area open for further explorations."
- **The defenders:** The ℓ-bit / LIOM picture (Serbyn–Papić–Abanin, PRL 110, 260601, 2013; Huse–Nandkishore–Oganesyan) posits emergent quasi-local integrals of motion giving strict localization. The rigorous anchor is Imbrie's proof (J. Stat. Phys. 163, 998, 2016; arXiv:1403.7837): a **conditional theorem** for a 1D random-field/random-exchange Ising chain in the strong-disorder regime, relying on an unproven "Limited Level Attraction" assumption about level statistics. It is rigorous *given* that assumption; it is not unconditional. (Imbrie himself frames it this way and notes recent numerics "have raised questions about whether there is a true MBL phase.")
- **The avalanche instability argument:** De Roeck–Huveneers; Morningstar, Colmenarez, Khemani, Luitz & Huse (Phys. Rev. B 105, 174205, 2022) — rare thermal inclusions ("bubbles") can spread and thermalize the whole chain unless disorder is very strong. Sels ("Markovian baths and quantum avalanches," arXiv:2108.10796; publ. Phys. Rev. B 106, L020202, 2022) states the critical disorder for avalanche stability of the canonical disordered Heisenberg chain "is shown to exceed W* ≳ 20," and that "the avalanche threshold drifts considerably with system size, with no evidence of saturation" — implying much of the numerically-studied "MBL" is only finite-size MBL. (An even larger threshold, ~80, is sometimes attributed to still-larger-system estimates, but I could **not** verify that specific figure in the abstract — treat as unconfirmed.)
- **The number-entropy sub-controversy:** Kiefer-Emmanouilidis, Unanyan, Fleischhauer & Sirker (PRL 124, 243601, 2020) reported that "in the regime which is expected to show many-body localization and where the entanglement entropy grows as S∼ln t, the number entropy grows as S_N∼ln ln t, indicating continuing subdiffusive particle transport at a very slow rate" — arguing against true localization. This was disputed by Luitz–Bar Lev (Phys. Rev. B 102, 100202, 2020) and by Ghosh–Žnidarič (resonance-induced growth, Phys. Rev. B 105, 144203, 2022). The most recent word favors the defenders: Aceituno Chávez, Artiaco, Klein Kvorning, Herviou & Bardarson (PRL 133, 126502, 2024) built an explicitly-MBL ℓ-bit model (exponentially localized ℓ-bits, exponentially decaying interactions) that *also* shows ultraslow, system-size-growing number entropy — concluding that "the observation of such growth in microscopic models is not sufficient to rule out many-body localization." This diagnostic therefore **cannot by itself decide the question**.
- **Experiments:** Superconducting-processor and cold-atom experiments observe MBL *phenomenology* but cannot access the asymptotic limit. Guo et al. (Nature Physics 17, 234, 2021) reported an energy-resolved MBL transition on a **19-qubit** programmable superconducting processor; Guo et al. (Stark-MBL, PRL 127, 240502, 2021) used **29 functional superconducting qubits** ("faithfully reproducing the relaxation dynamics of a nonintegrable spin model"; the arXiv:2011.13895 preprint says thirty-two, the published PRL says 29). Léonard, Kim, Rispoli, Lukin, Schittko, Kwan, Demler, Sels & Greiner (Nature Physics 19, 481, 2023) probed the onset of avalanches, finding evidence for accelerated transport of a thermal inclusion into the localized region, thermalizing it site by site. These inform but do not settle the phase question.

### Q2 — Page curve and replica wormholes: theorem vs conjecture

**The setup.** Hawking's 1976 calculation implied black-hole evaporation destroys information (entropy of radiation grows monotonically). Unitarity requires the "Page curve" (Page, PRL 71, 3743, 1993): radiation entropy should turn over at the Page time. The island formula, S(R) = min ext_I [Area(∂I)/4G_N + S_QFT(I∪R)], reproduces the Page curve.

**What is controlled vs conjectured (audited):**
- **Controlled calculation (derivation-level within a model):** In 2D Jackiw–Teitelboim (JT) gravity coupled to a CFT, the island rule is *derived* from the Euclidean gravitational path integral via replica wormholes. The two foundational papers are Penington, Shenker, Stanford & Yang ("Replica wormholes and the black hole interior," arXiv:1911.11977, JHEP 03 (2022) 205) and Almheiri, Hartman, Maldacena, Shaghoulian & Tajdini ("Replica Wormholes and the Entropy of Hawking Radiation," arXiv:1911.12333, JHEP 05 (2020) 013). Earlier: Penington ("Entanglement wedge reconstruction and the information paradox," arXiv:1905.08255) and Almheiri–Mahajan–Maldacena–Zhao ("The Page curve of Hawking radiation from semiclassical geometry," arXiv:1908.10996, JHEP 03 (2020) 149). This builds on the Lewkowycz–Maldacena (JHEP 08 (2013) 090) replica derivation of Ryu–Takayanagi. Reviewed in Almheiri, Hartman, Maldacena, Shaghoulian & Tajdini, "The entropy of Hawking radiation" (Rev. Mod. Phys. 93, 035002, 2021).
- **What is assumed, not proven:** (1) that the *gravitational path integral* is a well-defined object computing the correct fine-grained entropy; (2) the analytic continuation in replica number n→1; (3) **replica symmetry** of the dominant saddle (the derivation only needs the *saddle* to be replica-symmetric — weaker than assuming it everywhere — but it is still an assumption); (4) that the QES/island is the correct prescription (the quantum-extremal-surface prescription of Engelhardt–Wall generalizes RT/HRT and is itself derived from the same path-integral logic, not independently proven). In higher dimensions the island formula is *argued for* semiclassically (Penington) or *derived* only within doubly-holographic models where it reduces to standard RT — i.e., it inherits RT's conjectural status rather than being independently established.
- **The ensemble-averaging interpretation (the deep caveat):** In JT gravity, the connected wormhole geometries compute **ensemble-averaged** quantities — JT gravity is dual to a matrix integral (Saad–Shenker–Stanford), not a single boundary theory. So the "unitary Page curve" that islands reproduce is, in these solvable models, a statement about an *average* over theories, not necessarily a single black hole.

**The factorization puzzle and where unitarity stands (2023–2026):** Wormholes connecting disconnected boundaries threaten factorization (decoupled CFTs should have factorizing partition functions). Interpretations: (a) gravity computes an ensemble average (fine in JT); (b) factorization is restored by "half-wormhole" and α-state contributions (Saad–Shenker–Stanford–Yao, "Wormholes without averaging," JHEP 09 (2024) 133; Saad–Shenker–Yao, "Comments on wormholes and factorization," JHEP 10 (2024) 076; α-states in the Marolf–Maxfield model; Blommaert–Iliesiu–Kruthoff, "Gravity factorized," JHEP 09 (2022) 080). Crucially, **Schlenker & Witten ("No Ensemble Averaging Below the Black Hole Threshold," arXiv:2202.01372, JHEP 07 (2022) 143) argue that in D≥3 an appropriate ensemble of boundary theories does NOT exist**, and sub-threshold (non-black-hole) observables show no averaging effects. So the ensemble interpretation is understood to be a special feature of low-dimensional models, not a universal statement about quantum gravity. **Bottom line: the island/replica-wormhole program is a genuine advance — it shows the semiclassical path integral "knows" about the Page curve — but it does not by itself constitute a proof that a single evaporating black hole evolves unitarily.** Firewall (AMPS 2013) tensions are widely regarded as softened by the island/entanglement-wedge-reconstruction picture, but this is interpretation-dependent, not settled fact.

### Q3 — Area laws and the entanglement→geometry program

**Proven (theorems):**
- **1D gapped, local Hamiltonians:** Hastings ("An area law for one-dimensional quantum systems," JSTAT 2007, P08024; arXiv:0705.2024) rigorously proved an area law for the entanglement entropy of the ground state of a 1D gapped local Hamiltonian (entropy bounded, independent of chain length), and that this implies efficient matrix-product-state approximability. This is a genuine **theorem**. Bounds later improved by Arad, Kitaev, Landau & Vazirani (arXiv:1301.1162) to poly-logarithmic dependence on local dimension, and the exponential-clustering→area-law implication in 1D is also established (Brandão–Horodecki).
- **2D frustration-free, locally gapped:** Anshu, Arad & Gosset ("An area law for 2D frustration-free spin systems," STOC 2022; arXiv:2103.02492) proved an area law (with poly-log correction, entropy ≤ n^{1+O(1/polylog n)} for a length-n vertical cut) for a locally-gapped frustration-free 2D lattice. This is a **theorem** but is restricted to frustration-free, locally-gapped systems — not general gapped 2D Hamiltonians.

**Conjectural (open):**
- **The general area law in D≥2** for arbitrary gapped local Hamiltonians is an **open conjecture** (widely believed, unproven). Special cases (noninteracting/Gaussian — Gioev–Klich; matrix-product/PEPS-representable states) are known, but there is no general higher-dimensional proof. Even the 2D and quantum-PCP frontiers are noted as wide open in the complexity literature.

**The entanglement→geometry program — a sober taxonomy:**
- **Ryu–Takayanagi (PRL 96, 181602, 2006):** the statement that boundary entanglement entropy = Area of a bulk minimal surface / 4G. Originally a **conjecture** within AdS/CFT.
- **Lewkowycz–Maldacena (JHEP 08 (2013) 090):** provides a **controlled derivation** of RT from the bulk gravitational replica trick (assuming a replica-symmetric bulk saddle and a valid Euclidean path integral). This is derivation-level *within* semiclassical AdS/CFT — not a mathematical theorem about nature, since AdS/CFT itself is a (very well-supported) conjecture. The covariant HRT and quantum-corrected (Faulkner–Lewkowycz–Maldacena) versions extend it.
- **Tensor-network models (MERA — Swingle, Phys. Rev. D 86, 065007, 2012; HaPPY code — Pastawski–Yoshida–Harlow–Preskill, JHEP 06 (2015) 149; random tensor networks — Hayden, Nezami, Qi, Thomas, Walter & Yang, JHEP 11 (2016) 009):** these are **toy models** that concretely realize RT-like behavior and bulk reconstruction as quantum error correction. They are proofs of *concept/consistency*, not derivations that our universe's spacetime is built this way. As reviews note, RT in genuine string-theoretic AdS/CFT "remains ill-defined, or at least intractable in detail" because it is ultimately a statement about non-perturbative string theory.
- **Van Raamsdonk, "Building up spacetime with quantum entanglement" (Gen. Rel. Grav. 42, 2323, 2010; arXiv:1005.3035):** the **physical conjecture/slogan** that spacetime connectivity emerges from entanglement (disentangling boundary regions pinches off the bulk). This is a heuristic/interpretive proposal — influential, motivated by RT, but not a theorem. "ER=EPR" (Maldacena–Susskind 2013) is in the same category.

## Open Problems Ranked by Single-GPU / Colab Tractability

*(Each null-case is stated before the promise, per hygiene rules.)*

**Tier 1 — directly touchable with ED (L≈18–22) / MPS / Krylov on a single GPU:**

1. **Full-ETH free-cumulant structure in small spin chains.** *NULL-CASE:* if computed 4th-order free cumulants of local operators in a non-integrable chain do NOT reproduce the measured 4-time correlators (i.e., the non-crossing/free-probability decomposition fails at accessible L), the "full ETH" refinement is demoted for local systems. *Only then, the promise:* Pappalardi–Fritzsch–Prosen (2025) showed it works at exactly ED-accessible sizes, so replication and extension (new observables, frequency dependence, integrable vs chaotic contrast) is feasible and publishable.
2. **PXP scar revival fidelity and entanglement scaling vs perturbations.** *NULL-CASE:* if revival amplitude and the putative scar's sub-thermal entanglement do NOT show a systematic, extrapolable trend across L=16–22 — e.g., the scar's entanglement drifts monotonically toward the thermal Page value with L with no sign of a protected subspace — the "scars survive to the thermodynamic limit" reading is undercut. *Only then, the promise:* ED at these sizes is cheap and existing data (non-monotonic entanglement, outliers) already hints where the thermodynamic-limit problem bites; a clean scaling study is valuable either way.
3. **Finite-size drift of spectral diagnostics (r-statistics, spectral form factor, Thouless time) across the MBL crossover.** *NULL-CASE:* if the crossover disorder Wc(L) drifts steadily upward with L with no saturation (as the skeptics predict), that is evidence *against* a finite-Wc phase — a clean, publishable negative. *Only then, the promise:* this is exactly the Šuntajs/Sierant diagnostic, reproducible at L≤20 with random-matrix baselines.

**Tier 2 — feasible but sensitivity-limited:**

4. **Number-entropy growth after a quench in a disordered chain vs an explicitly-MBL ℓ-bit model.** *NULL-CASE:* if the microscopic-model number entropy grows *faster* than any exponentially-localized ℓ-bit model can reproduce, that would revive the Kiefer-Emmanouilidis destabilization argument. *Only then, the promise:* Aceituno Chávez et al. (2024) made the ℓ-bit comparison the decisive test, and ℓ-bit circuits are cheap to simulate to large L.
5. **Avalanche/thermal-inclusion seeding in small chains coupled to a small bath.** *NULL-CASE:* if a weakly-coupled thermal bubble fails to spread (relaxation rate stays below the bubble's many-body level spacing) at all accessible disorders, that supports MBL stability; if it always spreads, that supports instability — either is a result. *Only then, the promise:* tractable via the Morningstar/Sels Markovian-bath reduction, though finite-size reach limits how far the conclusion extrapolates.

**Tier 3 — accessible model, but the deep question is out of reach:**

6. **Small-N SYK numerics / JT-adjacent toy models for the Page curve.** *NULL-CASE:* small-N SYK spectral statistics failing to match the RMT "ramp" would signal a coding/model error, not new physics. *Then the honest verdict:* SYK at N≈20–34 Majoranas is ED-accessible and reproduces the ramp/plateau, but single-GPU compute **cannot** resolve the factorization puzzle or test ensemble-vs-single-theory — those are structural/analytic questions. **Out of reach** for the core controversy.
7. **HaPPY/random-tensor-network toy models of holographic entropy.** *NULL-CASE:* if a small HaPPY-code instance fails to satisfy RT for some boundary region, it is a bug (the property for perfect-tensor codes is known). *Then:* pedagogically valuable and cheap, but it tests the toy model, not nature — no bearing on whether spacetime "really" emerges from entanglement.

## Adjacent Fringe Claims to Steer Around

- **"Islands / replica wormholes prove black holes are unitary."** *Why fringe as stated:* overclaims. The derivation is model-specific (JT), computes ensemble averages in solvable cases, and rests on the assumed validity of the gravitational path integral. Strong evidence, not proof, for a single black hole.
- **"MBL is a rigorously established phase of matter."** *Why fringe:* Imbrie's proof is conditional (Limited Level Attraction assumption) and 1D-specific; the flagship 2025 review explicitly says the phase question is unresolved. Stating MBL existence as settled fact misrepresents the field.
- **"Spacetime IS entanglement / ER=EPR is established physics."** *Why fringe as stated:* these are physically-motivated conjectures/slogans (Van Raamsdonk 2010; Maldacena–Susskind 2013), supported by toy models, not theorems. Treating the slogan as demonstrated fact is unwarranted.
- **"Number entropy grows without bound, therefore MBL is dead."** *Why fringe now:* the 2024 ℓ-bit result (Aceituno Chávez et al., PRL 133, 126502) shows ultraslow unbounded number-entropy growth is *consistent* with MBL, so this inference is invalid.
- **Non-peer-reviewed "multi-fold massive gravity" reinterpretations of the QES/island** (blog/preprint-only) — *why fringe:* idiosyncratic framework outside the mainstream derivation, not adopted or cited by the core literature.
- **"Scars are a fundamentally new, fully-understood form of order."** *Why fringe:* the microscopic origin of PXP scars is explicitly still open; claims of a complete theory overstate the case.

## Recommendations

1. **Start with Tier-1 problem #1 or #2** (full-ETH free cumulants, or PXP scar scaling). Both are ED-accessible at L≤22, have 2024–2025 primary literature to benchmark against, and produce a clean positive-or-negative result. Write the NULL-CASE into the pre-registration before running.
2. **For MBL, commit to a diagnostic-drift study (Tier-1 #3) framed around the null hypothesis** that Wc(L) does not saturate. A clean demonstration of continued drift is a publishable negative that directly engages the Šuntajs/Sierant vs Imbrie/Huse divide — and a clean demonstration of saturation would be even more striking.
3. **Do NOT attempt to adjudicate the factorization puzzle or "spacetime from entanglement" numerically** — single-GPU compute cannot touch the structural questions. Use SYK/HaPPY toys only for pedagogy and code validation.
4. **Thresholds that would change the plan:** (a) if free-cumulant tests fail at L=20, pivot to characterizing *which* correlators break — that itself is interesting; (b) if scar entanglement clearly converges to a sub-thermal value with L, escalate to larger MPS/TDVP runs; (c) if number-entropy in the microscopic model provably exceeds the ℓ-bit envelope, that reopens the destabilization case and warrants a focused write-up.

## Caveats

- **Confidence grades:** ETH-as-generic-rule (high); scars exist and are reproducible (high); scar microscopic origin (open); MBL regime real (high); MBL *phase* existence (open — genuinely divided); island rule derived in JT (high, within the model); island rule resolves single-BH unitarity (low / interpretation-dependent); 1D and 2D-frustration-free area laws (theorems, high); general higher-D area law (open conjecture).
- **Theorems vs conjectures are flagged inline throughout.** Hastings 2007 and Anshu–Arad–Gosset 2022 are theorems; Imbrie 2016 is a *conditional* theorem; RT, the general higher-D area law, and "spacetime from entanglement" are conjectures; Lewkowycz–Maldacena and the island derivation are controlled calculations *within* a semiclassical/holographic framework, not model-independent proofs.
- **Inference vs citation:** All specific papers, authors, years, and venues above are *cited* from the literature. The *tractability rankings* and *null-case designs* in the Open Problems section are my own *inferences/judgments*, not claims from any single source. The reading that the field is "genuinely divided" on MBL and on ensemble-averaging is *cited* (Sierant et al. 2025; Schlenker–Witten 2022), not merely inferred.
- **Two flagged uncertainties:** (1) I could not locate a specific 2024–2026 review/rebuttal by Abanin, Huse, or Serbyn defending MBL with a directly quotable passage; the defenders' position is reconstructed from the ℓ-bit literature (Serbyn–Papić–Abanin 2013) and Imbrie 2016. (2) The claim that the avalanche stability threshold reaches W* ≳ 80 in larger systems could not be verified against the Sels abstract (which states W* ≳ 20); treat the larger figure as unconfirmed. The qubit counts are as published: 19 qubits for the energy-resolved MBL experiment (Guo et al., Nat. Phys. 17, 234, 2021) and 29 qubits in the published Stark-MBL PRL (127, 240502, 2021; its preprint says 32).
- I have not independently re-verified every digit of every arXiv identifier; where a number is quoted it matches search-result metadata, but confirm DOIs before formal citation.
